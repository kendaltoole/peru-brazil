This day is intentionally unplanned.
That's the whole point.

Things to Do

  • Ollantaytambo Archaeological Site – Massive Inca fortress and temple complex towering over town. One of the best-preserved sites in Peru. Go early for fewer crowds. Open 7:00 AM – 5:30 PM. Included with Boleto Turístico.
  • Pinkuylluna Hike – Short but steep climb (~30–45 min up) to ancient Inca storehouses across from the ruins. Free entry, incredible valley views. Best with a carrier for the toddler. View on AllTrails
  • Riverside Walk at Sunset – Flat, stroller-friendly path along the Patacancha River. Head out around 4:30–5:00 PM for golden light on the mountains and great photo ops of the fortress.
  • Wander the Old Town – Original Inca stone walls, water channels, and quiet cobblestone streets. Beautiful in the morning.
  • Pick Up Souvenirs – Local artisan shops around the plaza
  • Do Nothing – Sleep in, find a cafe with a mountain view, or just stay in and nap at the hotel. That's the whole point of this day.

Why Ollantaytambo Is Great for a Chill Day

This town is one of the most charming spots in the Sacred Valley:

  • Lower altitude than Cusco (~600m lower), so you'll feel more comfortable
  • Small, walkable town—no need for taxis or planning
  • Quiet streets with original Inca stone walls and water channels
  • Great local restaurants with courtyard seating
  • Mountain views everywhere you look
Tomorrow: You'll drive back to Cusco (~1.5–2 hours). No rush to prepare tonight—just rest and enjoy the valley.
