Day 5 – Week 1: Peru May 6

Chill in Ollantaytambo

"The secret to not over-traveling"

🧘

This day is intentionally unplanned.
That's the whole point.

Why This Day Matters

Buffer days are the key to successful family travel. They give you:

  • Recovery time after the Machu Picchu push
  • A chance to catch up on sleep
  • More time to enjoy Ollantaytambo at your own pace
  • Space for your toddler to just... be a toddler
  • No rushing to pack up and transfer while exhausted

If You Want Ideas (But No Pressure)

  • Sleep in and have a lazy breakfast
  • Wander the old Inca town streets—they're beautiful and quiet in the morning
  • Let your kid splash around or explore open plazas
  • Visit the Ollantaytambo ruins at a relaxed pace (skip if legs are still tired)
  • Pick up souvenirs from local artisan shops
  • Find a cafe with a mountain view and just sit
  • Just stay in and read/play/nap at the hotel

Why Ollantaytambo Is Great for a Chill Day

This town is one of the most charming spots in the Sacred Valley:

  • Lower altitude than Cusco (~600m lower), so you'll feel more comfortable
  • Small, walkable town—no need for taxis or planning
  • Quiet streets with original Inca stone walls and water channels
  • Great local restaurants with courtyard seating
  • Mountain views everywhere you look
Tomorrow: You'll drive back to Cusco (~1.5–2 hours). No rush to prepare tonight—just rest and enjoy the valley.
