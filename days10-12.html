Days 10–12 – Week 2: Brazil May 11–13

Jungle Life

3 Days at Your Jungle Lodge

Overview

Theme: One Activity Per Day

These three days follow a gentle, repeatable rhythm. The goal isn't to pack in activities—it's to let the jungle come to you while respecting nap schedules and toddler energy levels.

The Daily Rhythm

Morning (One Guided Activity)

Choose ONE activity for the morning when energy is highest. This is your "main event" for the day.

Midday (Lunch + Rest)

Return to the lodge for lunch, then mandatory quiet time. Nap, read, swim if there's a pool, or just decompress in your room.

Late Afternoon (Optional Gentle Activity)

If everyone's rested, do something low-key: a short boat ride, wildlife watching from the dock, or a plant identification walk.

Evening (Dinner + Early Bed)

Dinner at the lodge, maybe some stargazing or night sounds, then early to bed. The jungle wakes up early!

Kid-Friendly Activity Ideas

Mix and match across your 3 days. Don't try to do everything!

Canoe/Kayak Ride Paddle through flooded forest. Kids love being on the water.
Wildlife Spotting Monkeys, birds, caimans (from a safe distance), pink dolphins.
Jungle Walk Short trail walk to see plants, insects, and maybe sloths.
Piranha Fishing Many lodges offer this. Toddlers love the excitement (catch & release).
Visit Local Community Some lodges offer visits to riverside communities.
Night Walk/Boat Caiman spotting at dusk. Check if appropriate for your child.

Jungle Survival Tips

  • Bug spray: Apply liberally and often. DEET-based works best.
  • Hydration: The humidity is intense. Drink constantly.
  • Sun protection: Even under canopy, UV exposure adds up.
  • Clothing: Light, long sleeves and pants protect from bugs and sun.
  • Flexibility: Weather can cancel activities. Roll with it.
Remember: One activity per day is enough. The jungle itself is the entertainment—let your kid absorb it at their own pace.

What Your Toddler Will Love

  • Spotting monkeys (they're everywhere and hilarious)
  • Boat rides (water is endlessly entertaining)
  • Bugs and frogs (supervised discovery)
  • The sounds (howler monkeys, birds, rain on the roof)
  • Swimming (if the lodge has a pool—many do)
  • Just being in a "treehouse" environment
